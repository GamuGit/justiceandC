Crime Prevention Strategies:

Crime prevention focuses on stopping crime before it happens. One strategy is community policing, where police build positive relationships with neighborhoods. Another method is designing safer spaces, such as adding lighting, cameras, or secure locks. Schools and communities also offer programs that give young people positive activities and support. Education about making good choices is another important tool. When everyone works together, it becomes easier to prevent crime and create safer communities.

Crime Prevention Strategies:

Community policing – police work with neighborhoods to build trust and solve problems together. Good lighting and cameras making public areas safer by increasing visibility. After-school programs giving kids and teens positive activities to stay away from trouble. Education and job opportunities helping people build better futures so they don’t turn to crime. Neighborhood watch groups – community members looking out for each other. Rehabilitation programs helping people who committed crimes learn new skills and make better choices. Mental health and counseling services supporting people struggling with stress, trauma, or addiction. Strong family and community support positive relationships that guide people toward good decisions.
